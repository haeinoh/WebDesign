Seoul?

Seoul is the captial city of South Korea and the largest city. Seoul is in the northwest of South Korea and proper comprises 233.69 square miles with 9 miles roughly bisected into northern and southern halves by the Han River. The city houses 11.8 million people, and the metropolitan area has 25.6 million, which is just over half of the population of South Korea. Seoul is divided into 25 gu (districts), which ranges from 10 to 47 square killometers and populations under 140,000 to over 630,000.

History of Seoul

Seoul was first discovered by Baekjae, one of the Three Kingdoms of Korea in the 18 BC. Since then, Seoul was the capital of Joseon Dynasty from 1392 to 1910 under the name Hanseong. It was called Gyeongseong at the time of the Japanese occupation, and it was called the present name Seoul since 1945 after liberation. Seoul hosted the 1986 Asian Games, the 1988 Olympic Games and the 2002 FIFA World Cup.



Downtown Seoul

Seoul is divided into the North (called Gangbuk) and the South (called Gangnam) based on the Han River. Among them, Downtown Seoul is located in the center of the North side of the river.
Jongno-gu and Jung-gu are downtown Seoul, which is a very important and core area for Seoul's historical and tourist attractions. In Jongno area, most palaces are located and alos there are important goverment agencies. The Jung district is located in Seoul City Hall, and also home to Myeongdong and traditional markets, which are famous for shopping and sightseeing.
